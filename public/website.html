Research Domain

Explore the academic foundation and technical landscape of our research.

Literature Survey

A comprehensive review of existing research in Decision support system for reservoirs was conducted. We examined over 50 peer-reviewed papers from IEEE, Springer, and PubMed databases covering topics such as reservoir storage, inflow forecasting, flood affected area pediction, downstream crop water requirment, outflow prediction for decision support applied to reservoir and dam datasets.

Research Gap

Existing Mau-Ara Dam operations rely on static rule curves and historical data, lacking real-time predictive capabilities. Machine learning-based inflow forecasting and integrated optimization approaches remain underutilized. Current models also neglect uncertainty, climate variability, and multi-objective decision-making. Additionally, user-friendly, data-driven decision support systems tailored to small reservoirs like Mau-Ara are insufficient.

Research Problem

"Current reservoir operations are ongoing through engineers’ experience and some other manual calculations. Dam operations relying on static, rule-based decisions without accurate inflow forecasting, flood risk prediction, or real-time crop water demand estimation. The absence of an integrated, data-driven decision support system leads to inefficient water releases and increased operational risk "

Research Objectives

  • Develop a model that predict both inflow and current storage levels of Mau-Ara Dam.
  • Estimate water demand of the paddy cultivation based on growth stages, soil conditions and Weather.
  • To develop a machine learning model that predicts the likelihood and potential severity of downstream flooding caused by reservoir overflow or excessive rainfall in the Mau-ara catchment area, enabling early warning and proactive flood management actions.
  • Dam outflow prediction for decision support.

Methodology

Our research follows the Design Science Research Methodology (DSRM):

  1. Problem Identification & Motivation
  2. Objectives of the Solution
  3. Design & Development (Model Training & System Architecture)
  4. Demonstration (Prototype Testing)
  5. Evaluation (Clinical Expert Validation)
  6. Communication (Publication & Reporting)
